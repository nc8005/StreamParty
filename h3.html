Shared Streaming Feature for Netflix

Akira Kovacsics, Nicholas Capadano

ISTE 264 Project | Evaluation of Lo-fi Prototype

Design Problem Analysis (hw1) Figma Lo-Fi Prototype (hw2) Evaluation of Lo-Fi Prototype (hw3) Figma Hi-Fi Prototype (hw4)

Netflix no longer wants to support third-party apps for shared streaming. They want to design their own, native feature where users can start a host session, and invite friends to watch a show at the same time. This way, users won’t have to download third-party apps and be forced to trust other bootleg companies with their data. In addition, Netflix wants to discourage the sharing of accounts by multiple people, and wish to encourage subscription purchases by offering the feature only to those with their own, separate paid account.

Just look at the screen, and tell me what you think of it. Does anything come out to you upon first glance? Possibly about what you can do, what you like, what you don’t like, what’s unusual, or what’s familiar. Just don’t click anything yet.

Discussion

All participants said they used Netflix, and cited other streaming services such as HBO Max, Hulu, Amazon Prime, and AppleTV+. Most participants were very unfamiliar with using third-party applications to share streams, and did not use them. However, one participant said they used Netflix Party as a third party application, and another one mentioned the use of Shareplay for Facetime.

Netflix was the most popularly cited streaming service that people used, with all participants saying they used it the most. Participants rated themselves high on the scale for familiarity with streaming service interfaces (between 7-8), but low for familiarity with stream sharing features (all scored 4 or less). Unfortunately, all participants said that they predominantly use the desktop websites to stream tv and movies, but on the plus side, rated themselves highly on familiarity with Netflix and it’s interface (8-10).

The task that many had difficulty with was trying to join a Stream Party from the home page. There was a lot of time taken to find it in some cases, with one participant stating that the icon was difficult to find.

“I first looked down to the bottom panel–like, scanned those buttons to check them out, and it did take me a little bit to find the little people button–because it is pretty little. But once I saw it, I knew that it was definitely the one.”

On the plus side, this participant stated that the button’s function was clear once she found it. However, another participant thought differently.

“It’s a little bit unclear which of these two buttons is the stream party, I’m gonna guess it’s the one with people on it and the other one is Airplay or something.”

In terms of locating the participants tab, all participants found it fairly easy to figure out which button to click, because of the fact that the icon was consistent.

When leaving from the Stream Party, all participants tried to first leave from the regular exit button in the top right corner of the screen, when a video is playing. Notably, one participant saw that there was an option to exit from the participants panel, but misinterpreted the function of the button.

“I assume that End Session will end it for everyone, so I’m just gonna exit out of that.”

Another thing that many participants had trouble with was figuring out how to host a Stream Party. One needed guidance, and tried clicking the button that would make one join a Stream Party, which wouldn’t work to create one. It should be noted, however, that this participant stated that they never use Netflix on mobile, so may be very unfamiliar with the fact that users need to click on something to watch to bring up the information on it, and can actually play the content from this window.

“Once I was into the play section–like the choose your movie section, it did make sense, it was immediately once I was there, to find how to start it. But knowing to choose the movie before starting it was difficult.”

Another participant who ranked themself as very familiar with Netflix’s interface had much less trouble navigating to the page that would grant them access to the button that would allow them to start a Stream Party.

“There are two options that immediately come to mind–one is that I go to a show, and share it. The second option is to press on the same button as last time.”

“The only thing that was ambiguous was on the homepage, where there were the two buttons, on the top right. I’ve seen the other one, which I assumed was Airplay.”

Participants thought that once on the information page for a show or movie, finding the button to start a Stream Party was very easy to find, and said that it was intuitive.

There were no problems with participants figuring out how to kick other users from the stream, starting a stream as a host, or finding the participants tab. Ending the stream was similar as well, since the users already knew how to exit streams from navigating the guest side of the interface.

When prompted for suggestions, one participant said that they were unsatisfied with the pop-ups in the interface, and said that a full-screen overlay would be better.

“The only thing I thought was inconsistent was the end session button, and it popped up over the existing screen share section? [...] The way it popped up over the existing things, I thought was messy.”

Another participant said that making the Stream Party button on the home page would be useful. Notably, this same participant stated earlier in the testing that one thing they liked a lot about Netflix was the fact that their buttons were obvious.

As a follow-up, when questioned on the obviousness of the button, and whether or not text would be useful to include, they expressed confidence that adding text would not be necessary.

“It’s a pretty good universal symbol, with a little crowd of people. It’s the only time Netflix would use an icon like that, so I think it makes sense without any text.”

Another participant praised the fact that the Stream Share button for a host was accessible from outside the stream.

“I like the fact that there’s a button to do the stream before you start playing [the movie]. I like that you can start the stream at the same time, or before. I thought that was really nice and really thoughtful.”


Future Directions:

To move forward in the future, the next steps would consider the participant feedback and edit the interface as necessary.

One thing that should be done for the future would be to make the home screen easier to navigate. That is to say, finding the Stream Party button to join a stream should be made easier, so users will be able to find it better. The icon is fairly intuitive, as noted by participants, so the icon does not need much changing. However, possibly making the Stream Party more obvious on the interface (like the play button) or putting it in the bottom navigation bar may make it significantly easier to find.

Regarding the problem related to having trouble finding the option to start a Stream Party, and the fact that participants would first be inclined to try clicking the button to join a Stream Party when trying to find a way to host: it may be worthwhile to add additional text on the screen when the join dialogue pops up, explaining how to start your own Stream Party. This way, users will get instructions when they navigate to the only other Stream Party-related button they know, if they try to click it to start their own Stream Party.

Since a participant misinterpreted the exit function in the participants tab once the stream has started, it may be best to get rid of this alternative way to exit. There may not be a need to have two different ways to exit or stop a stream, and simplifying the process so it is consistent with the usual way people exit watching a show would be best.

Lastly, regarding the pop-ups, it may be best to get rid of them. After the testing, upon further review, the Netflix mobile app doesn’t actually use pop-ups. Therefore, introducing them may not align with Netflix’s design guidelines. Instead, the pop-ups should be replaced with other ways that Netflix brings up sub-menu options.
